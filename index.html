Given a line and a point not on the line, there exists exactly one line through the point that is parallel to the given line.

A group $(G, \cdot)$ is a set $G$ with a binary operation $\cdot$ satisfying the following properties:

  1. Closure: For all $a, b \in G$, $a \cdot b \in G$.
  2. Associativity: For all $a, b, c \in G$, $(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)$.
  3. Identity: There exists an element $e \in G$ such that $e \cdot a = a \cdot e = a$ for all $a \in G$.
  4. Inverse: For each $a \in G$, there exists an element $a^{-1} \in G$ such that $a \cdot a^{-1} = a^{-1} \cdot a = e$.

Every bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}^n$ has a convergent subsequence.

Let $(x_n)$ be a bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}^n$. Then there exists $M > 0$ such that $\|x_n\| \leq M$ for all $n \in \mathbb{N}$.
