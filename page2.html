We say that $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$ if for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that for all $x$ with $0 < |x - a| < \delta$, we have $|f(x) - L| < \varepsilon$.

Let $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}$ be continuous on $[a, b]$ and differentiable on $(a, b)$. Then there exists at least one point $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$.

Define the function $g(x) = f(x) - f(a) - \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}(x - a)$.

Then $g(a) = g(b) = 0$, and $g$ is continuous on $[a, b]$ and differentiable on $(a, b)$.

By Rolle's Theorem, there exists $c \in (a, b)$ such that $g'(c) = 0$.

But $g'(x) = f'(x) - \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$, so $g'(c) = 0$ implies $f'(c) = \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}$.

Consider $f(x) = x^2$ on $[0, 1]$. Then $f'(x) = 2x$ and $\frac{f(1) - f(0)}{1 - 0} = 1 - 0 = 1$.

If $f: [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}$ is continuous on the closed, bounded interval $[a, b]$, then $f$ attains both a maximum and minimum value on $[a, b]$.

Since $f$ is continuous on the compact set $[a, b]$, the image $f([a, b])$ is also compact in $\mathbb{R}$.

A compact subset of $\mathbb{R}$ is closed and bounded. A closed and bounded subset of $\mathbb{R}$ contains its supremum and infimum.
